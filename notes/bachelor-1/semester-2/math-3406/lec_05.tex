\begin{theorem}
	If \( AB \) is invertible, \( A,B \) is invertible.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
	if \( AB \) is invertible, there exists \( C = (AB)^{-1}  \) such that \( (AB)C = I \). Then, \( A(BC) = I \), and \( BC = A^{-1}  \).
\end{proof}
